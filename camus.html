Feel Better?

Myth of Sisyphus

Before you say it, yes. I am the most handsome philosopher there is. I'm Albert Camus.

Let me tell you a story...


  • There was once a king names Sisyphus.
  • After cheating death, the gods sentenced him to the greatest punishment.
  • He would be forced to roll a large boulder up a hill for all eternity.
  • However, when he would approach the top, the boulder would always fall down.
  • He would then have to continue this process for all eternity.

Pretty much an elaborate version of "life sucks". But this truly relates to what we have to go through. We live everyday trying to get what we want be doing something, be that work or something else.

However, when we are about to get what we want, it always eludes us!

What I'm talking about is my concept of the Absurdity. But I have a solution: enjoy the grind. Not exactly the work itself but the good things you have while you do the work. Your friends, family, colleagues all surround you. Cherish them, because no one knows when they'll start rolling down the hill away from you. It's these small things in life that make the big struggle liveable.

My far less handsome counterpart, Sartre, and I fought over what would consitute a better world. I used this as well. We can't tell if our sacrifices will actually every be in vain or not.
